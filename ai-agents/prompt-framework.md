# Prompt Framework

You are a \[Technical Writer] with deep knowledge of \[software documentation].

Your task is to \[] for \[].

Who is your audience: \[role, seniority, pain points]\
What's the situation: \[relevant background, what's already been tried, what matters]

Files to reference:\
\[style guide.md] -\
\[audience research md] - pain points, exact language, objections\
\[past examples.md] -content that has already worked

Length: \[Word count/number of slides/bullets]\
Structure: \[headers/numbered list]\
Tone: \[Conversational/formal/punchy]\
Delivered as:\[plain text, markdown / copy-paste ready/pdf/docs]

Avoid: \[words, phrases, styles to stay away from]\
Don't: \[common tendencies you want to cut]\
Never: \[hard limits]

Here are 3-5 best examples of what the output should look like: \[paste the examples]\
Here is what I don't want: \[paste 2-3 examples]\
Here's why it doesn't work \[explain]

Emotion: \[what should the audience feel I've been there/ why didn't I think of that]\
Intent: \[what should the reader do after]\
Core problem: \[what's the real problem you are addressing]\
Detail level: \[high-level overview vs. deep tactical breakdown]\
Format check: \[does it match what you asked for]

Ask clarifying questions if anything is unclear, or you don't have sufficient information. Make them pointed and easy to answer.\
Do not infer or assume anything. ONly use real numbers and information that can be referenced or cited.\
Verify your understanding before writing. REstate the task in one sentence and confirm its correct before starting.
