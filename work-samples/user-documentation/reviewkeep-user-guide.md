# ReviewKeep User Guide

### Login

Enter your username (email), password, and business. Then click **Sign In**. You will be directed to your dashboard.

_Note: Create a new account if you have not signed up yet._

### Header

This section includes the Search field, Location, Notifications, and User Profile.

#### Search Section

This is a system-wide search that lets you quickly find information. You can search by name, email, phone number, or call record data.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (2)" alt="" width="400"></div>

#### Location

This displays the business location you are currently viewing. If you have multiple locations and want to switch, click the location drop-down box. Additional locations will be shown. Then select the location you want to view.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (3)" alt="" width="400"></div>

After you choose a location, all related data for that location, including reviews, customers, and marketing, will be displayed.

_Note: Only users who have been given access to other locations by an administrator can view them in the Users setup section._

#### Notifications

The bell icon shows the number of reminders you need to review in a red circle. Click the bell ![](<../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (4)>) icon to view the notifications.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (5)" alt="" width="400"></div>

You can handle each notification separately. The reminder date and time appear next to the customer’s name. You can also see their contact information and the reason for the reminder.

Once you have handled the reminder or appointment, click the **Dismiss** button. Reminders will remain until you dismiss them.

#### User Name and User Image

The user name and image are displayed next to the notification icon. By default, ReviewKeep assigns a stock image when a person signs in to the system. You can change it to an actual photo or another avatar by using the **Upload User Image** option in the User Setup section.

### Business Dashboard

After you sign in, you are directed to your company dashboard. The dashboard includes several box-based sections, such as Header, Reviews Generated, Reviews Received, Competition Avg. Rating, Posts to Review Sites, Send Review Request, Customer Service, Recent Reviews, Review Sites, and 12 Month Average Review Request Rating.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (7)" alt="" width="400"></div>

#### Calendar Icon

By default, the calendar is set to **All**, which means it displays all data related to your reviews, ratings, customer service, and review posts since your subscription began.

If you want period-specific data, click the calendar icon and choose from preset time periods such as yesterday, today, this week, last week, last month, the last 90 days, or a custom date. Your dashboard will then reflect only the data for the selected dates.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (8)" alt="Calendar Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

#### Reviews Generated

Depending on the selected dates, this section displays all review requests sent by you and your team. For example, if you sent 8 review requests during that period, the value **8** will appear next to **Reviews Generated**.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (9)" alt="" width="400"></div>

Click **See Full Report** to open the Review Requests page. There, you can view all details about your review requests, including those received and those still pending.

#### Reviews Received

Depending on the selected dates, this section displays all reviews received from your review requests. These are requests that were sent to customers and then completed and returned. This section also shows the average rating of the reviews you have received.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (10)" alt="Graphical user interface, application Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

_Note: These are internal reviews only, not publicly posted reviews. Only positive reviews (4–5 stars) are prompted for public posting. They are tracked on the Review Requests page, which you can access directly or by clicking **See Full Report** in the dashboard box._

#### Competition Average Rating

Competition Average Rating shows the competitors you added in the Competitor section during initial setup. These are the two competitors you chose, if you completed that section, to compare against your ratings. Their star rating and number of reviews are shown in this box.

Tracking competitor ratings and review volume is helpful. Star ratings matter, but the number of reviews also matters because customers tend to trust businesses with more reviews.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (11)" alt="" width="400"></div>

#### Posts to Review Sites

This section shows reviews posted to your review sites.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (12)" alt="Graphical user interface, application Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

Google and Facebook show actual postings because they track these numbers correctly on the Review Requests page. Yelp does not allow this tracking. Because of that, the system makes a best guess by tracking whether the customer clicked the public posting link. For Yelp, that click is counted as a public post, but it does not confirm that the customer completed the post.

Yelp public posts must be verified manually. Open your Yelp business page and confirm whether those posts are live. Yelp also has a strict verification process, and unless a user has some history with Yelp, the review may be classified as **not trusted**.

#### Customer Service

This section shows how many review requests came back as 1, 2, or 3 stars. Industry standards generally treat these ratings as unfavorable for your review score. A 3-star review may be average, but it usually does not encourage people to visit or buy from you, so it is still considered a rating you do not want to promote publicly.

This section also shows how many customers you or your customer service team have contacted to address unsatisfactory experiences. If issues are resolved positively, the final number reflects how many have been resolved.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (13)" alt="" width="400"></div>

#### Recent Reviews

Recent Reviews shows your most recent individual reviews across all monitored review sites. For example, if Google, Yelp, and Facebook are all set up, the system monitors all of them and displays the most recent reviews from those sites.

These are sorted by recency. If the most recent reviews are all from Google, then this section will show only Google reviews. If two are from Google and one is from Yelp, that is what you will see on the dashboard. You will also see the reviewer’s name, the site where the review was posted, and the score they gave your business.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (14)" alt="Table Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

#### Review Sites

This section shows the overall score and number of reviews for each connected review site. If you have Google, Facebook, and Yelp accounts, you will see all three, along with each site’s average review score and total number of reviews.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (15)" alt="Table Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

#### Send Review Request

This is the core of the system. It is where you create review requests, collect feedback, and trigger public posting for positive reviews. If the review is not positive, it can be routed to your customer support team. When a customer arrives, ask for their name and contact information, such as a mobile number, email address, or both, and get their permission to send them an SMS, an email, or both.

**Customer Data Entry**

Enter the customer’s mobile number or email address, along with their first and last name, to start the review request.

Next, select the communication type based on the customer’s preference: Email, SMS, or both. This preference is then stored in the system and used for future communications unless it is changed later.

Click **Send**.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (16)" alt="Graphical user interface, text, application, email Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

This does two things. It sends the review request by SMS, email, or both, depending on your selection. It also adds the customer to the Customer (CRM) section of the ReviewKeep system. Once the customer is in the Customer section, you can track all communications and reviews, add notes, and create reminders.

See the [Customers](reviewkeep-user-guide.md#customers) section for full details on ReviewKeep CRM capabilities.

_Note: If the customer is a returning customer, their name may appear automatically as you start typing. The system recognizes email addresses, mobile numbers, first names, and last names. If the same number, email, or name appears, confirm that it is the same person before sending another review request. If they have reviewed recently, it is usually best to wait. The industry standard is to send a new review request once per year if the customer has already reviewed a product or service. However, if it is for a different product or service, sending another request is acceptable._

#### 12 Month Average Review Request Rating

This section shows a graph of your business ratings on a 1–5 scale over 12 months, based only on internally received reviews, not publicly posted reviews.

_Note: If you see a 0 (zero), it means you did not receive any review requests for that month. Either no requests were sent, or no one responded._

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (17)" alt="Chart Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

This section is available in the left pane. Select the drop-down next to **Reviews** to see the list of Review Requests, Review Site Monitoring, Customer Service, and Customers.

## Review Requests

The Review Requests section is where you track all company review requests sent to your customers. This section gives you an overview of requests sent, received, viewed, not viewed, top keywords from received requests, response tracking, and the ability to send manual reminders.

### Overview

This section gives you a quick snapshot of how many requests have been sent, received, read, and left unread, across both text messages and emails.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (19)" alt="" width="400"></div>

### Top Keywords

Top Keywords are words and phrases your customers use when responding to private review requests. These are not publicly posted. They are internal review request keywords only. The system identifies frequently used words and phrases and shows you the most common ones.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (20)" alt="" width="400"></div>

### Reviews Received

Reviews Received is your starting point for reviewing, analyzing, and acting on customer feedback. This section shows all reviews that customers completed and sent back. Each customer has a box showing their rating, comments, name, date, and review status.

For positive reviews, the status shows whether the review has been publicly posted. If it has been posted, a green box will display **Publicly Posted**, and no further action is needed. If it has not been posted, a red **Not Publicly Posted** box will appear.

For reviews that are not yet publicly posted, the automated reminder system sends up to three reminders based on the configured schedule, as long as automated reminders are enabled. They are enabled by default. If automated reminders are turned off, customers will not receive reminders unless you send them manually in the **Public Post Reminders** section at the bottom of the page.

If the review is poor, meaning 1–3 stars, a blue **Customer Service** box will appear. Click it to view next steps and track the customer service process for resolving the issue.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (21)" alt="" width="400"></div>

### Reviews Sent (Not Responded)

The **Not Responded** section under Reviews Sent shows all customer requests that have not yet been completed. This section shows the customer name, the date sent, and whether reminders have been sent through the automated system or manually.

You can also see how each request was sent, whether by SMS, email, or both. You can check whether the customer has viewed the request. To send a manual reminder, select the checkbox next to the customer’s name and click the blue **Send Reminder** button at the top of the column.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (22)" alt="Graphical user interface, text, application, chat or text message Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

### Public Post Reminders (Not Responded)

The **Not Responded** section under Public Posts shows all customers who have not yet publicly posted their positive reviews, meaning 4- and 5-star reviews. Since these are your strongest reviews, you will usually want to encourage as many public posts as possible.

This section shows the customer name, the date the positive review was received, and whether reminders have been sent through the automated system or manually.

You can also see how each request was sent, whether by SMS, email, or both. You can check whether the customer viewed the request. To send a manual reminder, select the checkbox next to the customer’s name and click the blue **Send Reminder** button at the top of the column.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (23)" alt="Graphical user interface, application Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

## Review Site Monitoring

The Review Site Monitoring page tracks all publicly posted reviews. These reviews may come from customers responding to your review requests, or from customers who went directly to your review sites and posted independently.

These are the reviews visible to the public. It is important to track public posts and respond to them. Negative reviews especially should be answered with an explanation of the issue or an appropriate apology. Depending on your company policy, some businesses also offer refunds, replacements, or other corrective actions for poor experiences.

### Overview / Banner Section

This section provides a summary of your public reviews. It shows the average rating and total number of reviews across all monitored sites. It also shows a recommendation percentage based on rating, along with keywords associated with your business.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (24)" alt="" width="400"></div>

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (26)" alt="" width="400"></div>

**Sources** lets you filter by review site, so you can view all sources or only the sites you want to include.

These are the publicly posted reviews pulled from high-traffic review sites such as Google, Yelp, and Facebook. This section shows the customer name, review score, review date, and review comments.

For Yelp, you cannot respond directly within the ReviewKeep app because of Yelp policies. When you click the blue **Respond** icon, you will be directed to the Yelp business website.

## Customer Service

Customer Service is where you can view all customers who responded to company review requests with a 1-, 2-, or 3-star review. These are private internal reviews, not public reviews. The ReviewKeep system automatically classifies these ratings as poor and routes them to Customer Service.

This section allows you or your assigned customer service representatives to review and respond to customers quickly, so you can address their concerns before they go public with their comments. Fast responses matter. The sooner you communicate with customers, the less likely they are to post independently on public sites. The Customer Service section helps you resolve dissatisfied customer issues quickly.

### Overview

This section gives you a quick snapshot of how many customer service tickets have been received, resolved, contacted, are in progress, or are marked as disgruntled. In the snapshot below, you can see how your customer service team is performing.

## Customers

The Customers section is a CRM for your customer information. It tracks review requests, cards, coupons, call records, contact information, dates of birth for birthday cards or coupons, and reminders for upcoming events.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (45)" alt="" width="400"></div>

### Header Section

The top of the Customers section includes customer information and several available actions. First is the **Customer Name** column, which shows all customers in your company list as you scroll. The next area is a master search that lets you search all customer information, including names, contact information, and call records. The drop-down menu lets you search across all customer information or narrow the search to specific fields.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (46)" alt="" width="400"></div>

A customer is added automatically when you send a review request from the dashboard. If you want to add a customer without sending a review request, click the **Add Customer** button.

_Note: Other information, such as date of birth, mailing address, or customer image, can also be updated here by using the **Add Customer** option._

#### Adding a Customer

Click the **Add Customer** button. The data entry fields will appear.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (47)" alt="Graphical user interface, application Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

Fill in the available fields, upload an image if needed, and then click **Save**. The customer is saved to the system, and all entered information becomes searchable.

#### Customer Upload / Import

Customer Upload lets you import customers in bulk, so you do not have to enter them one by one. This can be done if your customer list is in an Excel spreadsheet in `.xlsx` or `.csv` format. ReviewKeep requires customer fields to be placed in specific columns, so download the template from the Customer Upload section first.

## Marketing

The Marketing section of ReviewKeep lets you send cards and coupons to your customers.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (57)" alt="" width="188"></div>

### Coupons & Cards

Coupons & Cards can be found under the Marketing drop-down menu. Cards can be used for any occasion, and templates are available for holidays, birthdays, and anniversaries. Coupons can be sent as percentage-off or dollar-off discounts for products or services.

There are two modes for both cards and coupons: **Basic** and **Advanced**. Basic mode uses built-in templates. Advanced mode allows you to create your own designs.

Your subscription level determines how many cards or coupons you can send each day. Silver allows 10 per day, Gold allows 100 per day, and Platinum allows 1000 per day.

#### Selecting Cards or Coupons

Click **Cards & Coupons** from the Marketing drop-down menu.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (58)" alt="" width="188"></div>

You will be directed to the **Coupons & Cards – Select Coupon/Event Card** page.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (59)" alt="Graphical user interface Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

Select the **Coupon** radio button and click **Next**. You will be directed to the **Coupons & Cards – Edit Coupon** page.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (60)" alt="" width="400"></div>

By default, the **Default** radio button is selected. This is the easiest option to use.

#### Customizing the Coupon

First, enter the **Coupon Title**, such as **20% off Storewide** or **$1 off any Coffee**. This tells the customer what the coupon offers.

Next, enter your message in the **Customer Message** field.

Select **Discount Amount** as **Percentage** or **Dollar**, depending on the type of discount you want to offer, and then enter the amount in the **Enter Amount** box.

Select both the start and end dates for the coupon validity period.

Choose a template from the **Background** section and click **Next**. After you complete the fields, the coupon is generated and a preview is displayed on the other side.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (61)" alt="" width="400"></div>

Click **NEXT**. You will be directed to the **Gift Cards and Events - Select Customer(s) and Send** page.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (62)" alt="" width="400"></div>

Select the desired customers from the list. If needed, use the search bar to find a specific customer. Once all customers are selected, click **Send** to send the coupon to the selected customers.

<div align="left"><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Unknown image (63)" alt="Graphical user interface, application Description automatically generated" width="400"></div>

#### Cards
